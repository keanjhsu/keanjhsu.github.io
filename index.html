Biography

Kean J. Hsu is a post-doctoral research fellow in the Mood Disorders Laboratory in UT-Austin’s Institute of Mental Health Research. He studies how dysfunction in basic cognitive processes, like attention and executive functioning, contribute to the development and maintenance of depression and related psychopathology (e.g., anxiety).

He is also interested in predictors and mediators of psychotherapeutic response for depression and anxiety in naturalistic settings, and how to increase awareness of issues surrounding mental health and stigma in communities that are typically under-served. He is co-director of the Anxiety and Stress Clinic with Jasper Smits, providing clinical supervision to trainees on empirically-supported treatments for emotional disorders.

Interests

  • Role of basic cognitive processes in depression and anxiety risk and maintenance
  • Cognitive biases
  • Repetitive negative thinking
  • Improving intervention delivery for depression and anxiety
  • Open science and replicability
